Prime Focus Telescope

This project was started to produce a (mostly) 3D printable prime-focus telescope for personal use, but evolved into an open-source project, published on Thingiverse with a bill of external materials and build instructions. The telescope is a prime focus Newtonian style reflector with an f8 mirror. It features a CMOS camera in place of a traditional eye piece, allowing for digital viewing and live capture for astrophotography. The telescope was design with simplicity and modularity in mind, featuring few pieces and a designed that supports optical flexibility. The main body is connected to top collector via steel tubing, held in place by a friction lock system, allowing the physical focal to be adjusted to any desired length. This combined with a modular mirror tray system allows the telescope to switch between planetary and deep space optical requirements. The telescope also features a simple collimator and a fine focus mechanism. This project was a great exercise in tolerance design (3D printed parts mating with each other and other components), overall design consideration (thinking about ease of construction and use), and design iteration (ex. first/proof of concept light collector was made from an old stripped-down web camera).
